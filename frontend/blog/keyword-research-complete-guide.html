What is Keyword Research?

Definition

Keyword research is the process of finding and analyzing search terms that people enter into search engines. The goal is to understand what your target audience is looking for, how often they search for it, and how difficult it is to rank for those terms.

Discover

Find what your audience is searching for

Analyze

Evaluate competition and search volume

Target

Choose the best keywords to pursue

Types of Keywords

1 Short-Tail Keywords (Head Terms)

1-2 words, high search volume, high competition

"shoes" "coffee" "insurance"

2 Mid-Tail Keywords

2-3 words, moderate volume, moderate competition

"running shoes for men" "organic coffee beans" "car insurance quotes"

3 Long-Tail Keywords

4+ words, lower volume, lower competition, higher conversion

"best running shoes for flat feet men" "organic fair trade coffee beans online"

4 LSI Keywords (Latent Semantic Indexing)

Related terms that provide context to search engines

"sneakers" (for shoes) "espresso" (for coffee)

Understanding Search Intent

Search intent (or user intent) is the reason behind a searcher's query. Google prioritizes content that matches intent.

Informational Intent

User wants to learn something

"how to tie a tie" "best SEO practices" "what is javascript"

Navigational Intent

User wants to find a specific website

"facebook login" "youtube" "gmail sign in"

Transactional Intent

User wants to buy something

"buy nike shoes online" "best price laptop" "cheap flights to nyc"

Commercial Investigation

User is comparing options

"nike vs adidas" "best laptop 2024" "seo tool reviews"

Keyword Metrics Explained

Search Volume

The average number of times a keyword is searched per month.

High Volume

10,000+

High competition

Medium Volume

1,000-10,000

Moderate competition

Low Volume

100-1,000

Lower competition

Keyword Difficulty (KD)

A score (0-100) estimating how hard it is to rank for a keyword.

0-20

Easy to rank

21-40

Moderate difficulty

41-60

Competitive

61-100

Very difficult

CPC (Cost Per Click)

How much advertisers pay per click for this keyword in Google Ads. Higher CPC generally indicates higher commercial intent.

$5+

High commercial value

$1-5

Moderate commercial value

Under $1

Lower commercial value

Keyword Research Tools

Google Keyword Planner

Free tool within Google Ads for basic keyword research

Best for: Beginners, budget-conscious

Pros: Free, official data

Cons: Limited features, ranges for volume

Ahrefs

Comprehensive SEO suite with powerful keyword research

Best for: Professionals, serious SEOs

Pros: Accurate data, competitor insights

Cons: Expensive

SEMrush

All-in-one marketing toolkit with keyword magic tool

Best for: Marketing teams, agencies

Pros: Feature-rich, accurate data

Cons: Premium pricing

Answer The Public

Visualizes questions people ask around your keywords

Best for: Content ideation

Pros: Visual, finds questions

Cons: Limited free searches

Screaming Frog

Website crawler that identifies keyword opportunities

Best for: Technical SEO, audits

Pros: Deep site analysis

Cons: Learning curve

Ubersuggest

User-friendly keyword research with content ideas

Best for: Small businesses, beginners

Pros: Easy to use, affordable

Cons: Less accurate data

Long-Tail Keyword Strategy

Why Long-Tail Keywords Win

  • Lower competition (easier to rank)
  • Higher conversion rates
  • More specific search intent
  • Better voice search optimization
  • Easier to create targeted content
  • Less expensive for PPC campaigns
