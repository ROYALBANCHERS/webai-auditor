1. What is Local SEO?

Local SEO (Local Search Engine Optimization) is the process of optimizing your online presence to attract more business from relevant local searches. These searches take place on Google and other search engines, with a focus on geographic proximity.

Why Local SEO Matters

  • 46% of Google searches have local intent
  • 72% of consumers who did a local search visited a store within 5 miles
  • 78% of local mobile searches result in an offline purchase
  • 88% of local searches on mobile result in a call or visit within 24 hours

Local Search Results Components

  • Map Pack: Top 3 local businesses shown on map
  • Local Business Listings: Business information cards
  • Localized Organic Results: Regular rankings with local focus
  • Google Maps: Detailed business information and directions

Key Insight:

Local SEO competition is often lower than organic SEO. With proper optimization, small businesses can outrank larger competitors in their local area. Use WebAI Auditor to analyze your local competition.

2. Google Business Profile Optimization

Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the most important factor for local SEO rankings. A complete and optimized profile is essential.

Setting Up Your Profile

  1. Claim or verify your listing: Go to google.com/business
  2. Choose primary category: Select the most relevant business category
  3. Add business name: Use your exact business name (no keywords)
  4. Verify location: Use postcard or video verification
  5. Complete all information: Hours, contact, attributes

Essential Profile Elements

Business Name

  • Use your official business name exactly as it appears in the real world
  • Avoid adding keywords or location to the name
  • Keep it consistent across all citations

Business Categories

  • Choose the most specific primary category
  • Add secondary categories if relevant (up to 9 total)
  • Categories should reflect actual services offered

Business Hours

  • Set accurate regular and special hours
  • Update hours for holidays and special events
  • Use special hours for holidays, events, or seasons

Business Description

  • Write a compelling 750-character description
  • Include unique selling proposition and key services
  • Naturally include relevant keywords
  • Avoid keyword stuffing or excessive repetition

Attributes

  • Select all relevant attributes (wheelchair accessible, women-led, etc.)
  • Keep attributes updated as business changes

Google Posts

Google Posts appear in your Business Profile and can enhance visibility:

  • What's New: General business updates and news
  • Offers: Promotions, coupons, and sales
  • Products: Showcase specific products
  • Events: Upcoming events and activities

Google Posts Best Practices

  • Post weekly to maintain freshness
  • Use high-quality images (750x750 pixels minimum)
  • Include clear call-to-action buttons
  • Write compelling headlines
  • Posts expire after 7 days (except events and offers)

Q&A Section

  • Answer common customer questions
  • Use questions as additional ranking opportunities
  • Keep answers concise but thorough
  • Add relevant questions from customers

3. Local Citations & Directory Listings

Citations are mentions of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) on other websites. Consistent NAP information across the web is crucial for local SEO.

NAP Consistency

  • Name: Exactly as it appears on your signage
  • Address: Complete and formatted correctly
  • Phone: Primary local phone number
  • Website: Consistent URL (with or without www)

Top Citation Sources

Primary Data Aggregators

These four aggregators feed data to hundreds of other sites:

  • Neustar Localeze
  • UberMedia (formerly Axciom/Factual)
  • Infogroup
  • Foursquare

Essential Directories

  • Google Business Profile
  • Bing Places for Business
  • Yelp
  • Apple Maps (via Maps Connect)
  • Facebook
  • Yellow Pages
  • Better Business Bureau
  • Chamber of Commerce

Industry-Specific Directories

  • Restaurants: TripAdvisor, OpenTable, Zomato
  • Home Services: Angi, Thumbtack, Porch
  • Medical: Healthgrades, Vitals, WebMD
  • Legal: Avvo, Justia, Lawyers.com
  • Automotive: Cars.com, Edmunds, RepairPal

4. Review Management Strategy

Online reviews are a critical local ranking factor. Both the quantity and quality of reviews impact your local search visibility.

Why Reviews Matter

  • Ranking factor: Reviews directly impact local rankings
  • Click-through rate: Star ratings appear in search results
  • Consumer trust: Reviews influence purchase decisions
  • Feedback: Reviews provide valuable customer insights

Getting More Reviews

  • Ask satisfied customers directly for reviews
  • Send follow-up emails after purchases
  • Create review request handouts
  • Display QR code linking to review profile
  • Make it easy with direct links
  • Timing matters: ask soon after service completion

Review Response Strategy

  • Respond to ALL reviews: Both positive and negative
  • Be timely: Respond within 24-48 hours
  • Be professional: Even with negative reviews
  • Personalize: Use the reviewer's name and specific details
  • Address issues: Offer solutions for problems raised
  • Thank customers: Show appreciation for positive feedback

5. On-Page Local SEO

Optimize your website pages for local search intent.

Title Tags for Local SEO

  • Include city/region name
  • Include primary keyword
  • Mention business type if appropriate
  • Keep under 60 characters

Example:

Best Pizza Restaurant in Chicago | Giorno's Pizza

Meta Descriptions

  • Include location and service
  • Mention unique selling points
  • Include call-to-action
  • Keep under 160 characters

NAP on Website

  • Display NAP prominently on contact page
  • Include NAP in footer or sidebar
  • Use schema markup for NAP data
  • Ensure NAP matches GBP exactly
  • Include embedded Google Map

Location Pages

For businesses with multiple locations, create individual pages:

  • Create unique page for each location
  • Include location-specific NAP
  • Add embedded map for each location
  • Include location-specific content and testimonials
  • Optimize for "near me" searches
  • Avoid duplicate content across location pages

6. Local Content Strategy

Create content that targets local search queries and demonstrates local expertise.

Content Ideas for Local SEO

  • Local guides: "Best parks in [city]"
  • How-to guides: "How to choose a plumber in [city]"
  • Local news: Cover local events and news
  • Case studies: Local client success stories
  • Community involvement: Sponsorships and partnerships

Location-Based Blog Content

  • Create content about local events and activities
  • Highlight community involvement
  • Showcase local projects and clients
  • Provide local resources and information
  • Address local customer pain points

7. Local Link Building

Build links from locally-relevant websites to boost local authority.

Local Link Opportunities

  • Local business associations: Chamber of Commerce
  • Local media: Newspapers, TV stations, radio
  • Local blogs: City and neighborhood blogs
  • Sponsorships: Local events, teams, charities
  • Partnerships: Other local businesses
  • Local directories: City and industry-specific

Link Building Strategies

  • Sponsor local events and charities
  • Participate in community activities
  • Offer scholarships or internships
  • Host local workshops or events
  • Create local resources or tools
  • Get involved with local government initiatives

9. Tracking Local SEO Performance

Measure the effectiveness of your local SEO efforts.

Key Metrics to Track

  • Map Pack rankings: Position in local results
  • Organic rankings: Position in regular results
  • Visibility score: GBP profile completeness
  • Call clicks: Calls from GBP listing
  • Direction requests: Navigation requests
  • Website visits: Traffic from local searches

Tools for Local SEO Tracking

  • Google Business Profile insights: Built-in analytics
  • Google Search Console: Performance and ranking data
  • Google Analytics: Traffic and conversion tracking
  • Third-party tools: BrightLocal, Whitespark, Moz Local
